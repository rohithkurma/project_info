Project Description

Eye Blink Detection System is an innovative project designed to automatically detect eye blinks in EEG signals. By combining advanced signal processing techniques with machine learning, the project enhances the accuracy and reliability of blink detection. The system processes raw EEG data to remove noise, identifies potential blinks, and uses algorithms to differentiate true blinks from false positives. This automated approach provides a highly effective solution for real-time blink detection, making it valuable for various applications in neuroscience and beyond.

RESULTS

The protocol devised through this Blink Detection design provides a fully automated, unsupervised system for identifying eye-blinks in EEG data, in order to get over these restrictions. Although no user training or human inspection is required as it self-learns each user's unique brainwave profiles for eye-blinks, further research is required as no system is ideal. Just as much as humans are different in capacities, performance and mobility are different, the system would need to adapt to a more wider and extensive scope of users for a more close to accurate result. Furthermore, to assess the resilience of the system over a range of EEG headsets, user activities, and eye-blink kinds, we gathered distinct EEG datasets and according to the experimentation, the system consistently achieved more than 70% accuracy with an average precision of 0.821.

We assessed the algorithm's performance on involuntary eye blinks. The findings are shown as cumulative distributions for both platforms. With 98.96% accuracy for OpenBCI and 99.2% accuracy for Emotiv, the mean accuracy for all 20 participants. For both devices, the mean accuracy was higher than 0.9: 0.913 for Emotiv and 0.951 for OpenBCI. For OpenBCI, the top-5 and bottom-5 accuracy scores were 1.0 and 0.858, while for Emotiv, they were 0.993 and 0.801.
